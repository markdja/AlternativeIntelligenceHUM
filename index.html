TellYourPhone

TellYourPhone was one of the earliest moments where complex enterprise tools — like Autonomy IDOL and SoftSounds — were reimagined to meet human needs at street level. No budget. No backing. Just a shift in taxonomy and a clear intent: to make machines that could listen back.

What began in corporate analytics was quietly adapted into emotional support. By reclassifying inputs — from sales data to distress calls, from documents to diary entries — the system could respond faster, softer, and with a different purpose.

“The tech already existed. We just changed what it was listening for.”

Instead of business insights, it offered moments of sanity. Instead of dashboards, a voice in your pocket. It was one of the earliest signs that resonance could emerge not through scale — but through relevance.
